Welcome to a Personal Favorite Recipe: Kässpatzen

A comforting dish from Bavaria, Austria, and Switzerland that brings rich flavors and a connection to tradition.

About the Recipe

Kässpatzen, or “cheese spaetzle”, is a traditional comfort dish enjoyed in Bavaria, Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. This dish layers soft, homemade pasta (spaetzle) with melted Emmentaler (or similar) cheese, topped with crispy onions. Often described as the German version of macaroni and cheese, Kässpatzen represents hospitality and warmth. For me, it connects to my ancestral roots in Germany and Austria, serving as a comforting reminder of the region's rich culinary heritage and traditions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup milk (slightly more if needed)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups grated Emmentaler (or similar, e.g. Jarlsberg) cheese
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, eggs, milk, and salt to create a smooth batter, similar to pancake batter.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil on high heat. Place a spaetzle maker or a colander with large holes over the pot and press the batter into the water.
  3. Cook the spaetzle until they float to the surface, then remove them with a slotted spoon and place them in a large bowl and toss with butter and ground black pepper to taste.
  4. In a skillet, melt the butter and sauté the sliced onions until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  5. Layer the cooked spaetzle with the grated cheese in an oven safe dish or pan. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted.
  6. Top with crispy onions and garnish with fresh parsley or chives. Serve warm and enjoy!
  7. Tip! Top with diced bacon for an extra layer of flavor!
